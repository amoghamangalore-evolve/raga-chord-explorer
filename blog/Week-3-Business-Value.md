## Chapter 1: The Business Case - Why Build This?

### The Traditional Approach

**Scenario:** You're a PM with an idea for a music tool.

**Steps required:**

**Month 1: Planning & Design**
- Write detailed requirements doc (40 hours)
- Create wireframes and mockups (60 hours)
- Review with stakeholders (20 hours)
- Revise based on feedback (20 hours)
- **Total: 140 hours, 1 month**

**Month 2: Resource Allocation**
- Request development resources
- Wait for sprint capacity
- Negotiate priorities
- Get assigned to roadmap
- **Total: 1 month (mostly waiting)**

**Month 3-5: Development**
- Sprint 1: Setup and architecture (2 weeks)
- Sprint 2-3: Core features (4 weeks)
- Sprint 4: Audio engine (2 weeks)
- Sprint 5: UI polish (2 weeks)
- Sprint 6: Testing and fixes (2 weeks)
- **Total: 12 weeks, 3 months**

**Month 6: QA & Deployment**
- QA testing (2 weeks)
- Bug fixes (2 weeks)
- Deployment setup (1 week)
- Production deployment (1 week)
- **Total: 6 weeks, 1.5 months**

**Grand Total: 6.5 months, 6-person team**

**Scenario:** Same PM, same idea.

**Week 1: Conception & Prototyping**
- Day 1: Requirements with AI (4 hours)

**Week 2: Production Migration**

## Chapter 7: The Hidden Costs - What People Don't Talk About

### Costs AI Doesn't Eliminate

**1. Learning Curve**
- Time to understand AI collaboration: ~10 hours
- Learning Next.js concepts: ~20 hours
- Understanding TypeScript basics: ~15 hours
- **Total: ~45 hours of learning**

**For me:** Worth it (now I have these skills)
**For a non-technical person:** Might be overwhelming

**2. Debugging Time**
- AI makes mistakes
- I spent ~15 hours debugging issues AI created
- Had to learn to read/understand code to debug

**This isn't autopilot. You need to stay engaged.**

**3. Mental Overhead**
- Context switching (PM to pseudo-developer)
- Staying focused on product vs getting lost in code
- Knowing when to stop polishing

**It's easy to over-engineer when you have AI generating code quickly.**

**4. Opportunity Cost**
- 98 hours I could've spent on other things
- For a business, that's 2.5 weeks of PM time
- Could've managed 2-3 other projects instead

**Trade-off:** Build one thing yourself vs manage multiple things with teams
